## Phase 1: FOUNDATION (Months 1-2)
### "Load the gun before you fire it"

The #1 mistake marketplace startups make is spending money on customers before they have enough supply. We solve the chicken-and-egg problem by getting salons first.

### 1.1 — The "First 200" Salon Blitz

**Goal:** 200 salons signed up before spending a penny on customer acquisition.

**Strategy: Feet on the ground + direct outreach.**

We already have the database infrastructure from Colver Construction's CRM work. Apply the same approach:

**Week 1-2: Build the target list**
- Scrape every salon, hairdresser, nail tech, and beauty professional within 50 miles of your base (Warwickshire outward)
- Use Google Maps, Yell, Instagram, Facebook Pages
- Target: 2,000 beauty businesses identified with name, phone, email, Instagram
- Prioritise: Independent operators and sole traders NOT already on Fresha/Treatwell (that's 55-75% of the market)

**Week 3-8: Direct outreach campaign**

Channel 1 — **Instagram DMs** (highest conversion for beauty):
```
Hi [name] 👋 Love your work! I'm launching One Touch Beauty — 
a new UK booking platform for beauty pros. Unlike Fresha/Treatwell, 
we charge just 10% commission (they charge 20-35%). 

We're inviting the best local pros to join for FREE before we 
launch to the public. Would you be interested in a quick look? 
Takes 5 mins to set up 💅
```
- Send 50-100 per day from a dedicated business Instagram account
- Target: 5-10% response rate = 5-10 sign-ups per day

Channel 2 — **Walk-in visits** (highest trust):
- Visit salons in person with a one-page flyer and live demo on your phone
- Target high streets in Rugby, Coventry, Leicester, Solihull, Birmingham
- Aim for 5-10 salon visits per day
- Bring a tablet showing the platform live

Channel 3 — **Facebook Groups**:
- Join every UK beauty professional Facebook group (there are hundreds)
- "UK Hair & Beauty Professionals", "Salon Owners UK", "Self-Employed Beauty Therapists"
- Share value first (tips, advice), then soft-pitch the platform
- DO NOT spam — provide genuine value

Channel 4 — **Email outreach**:
- Use the scraped database
- Personalised emails referencing their specific salon
- Offer: "List free for the first year, no commission on your first 50 bookings"

**Key messaging to salons:**
> "Fresha charges 20% on new clients. Treatwell charges 35%. We charge 10%. Same features, a third of the cost. And the first year is free."

This undercut positioning is how Fresha killed Treatwell's growth — they offered free software when Treatwell charged subscriptions. We do the same to Fresha.

### 1.2 — The Founding Salon Program

Create an exclusive "Founding Salon" tier:
- **Free for life** (no subscription, no commission for 12 months)
- **"Founding Salon" badge** on their profile (social proof)
- **Priority support** (WhatsApp group with you directly)
- **Input on features** (they help shape the product)
- Limited to **first 200 salons only** (scarcity drives urgency)

This costs you nothing but creates evangelists who'll recruit other salons for you.

### 1.3 — Content Factory Setup

Before launching any campaigns, build the content machine:

**Create these accounts:**
- Instagram: @onetouchbeauty
- TikTok: @onetouchbeauty
- YouTube: @onetouchbeauty
- Facebook Page + Facebook Group ("One Touch Beauty — UK Pros")
- LinkedIn (for B2B/salon owner content)

**Content bank (create 30 pieces before launch):**
- 10 x "Salon spotlight" posts featuring your Founding Salons

- 5 x "How it works" walkthrough videos
- 5 x "Why we built this" founder story content

**Cost: £0** (you create this with your phone + Canva/CapCut)

## Phase 2: LAUNCH (Month 3)
### "Make noise, create FOMO"

### 2.1 — The Launch Campaign: "Beauty, One Touch Away"

**Launch week strategy — a 7-day event, not a single post.**

**Day 1 (Monday): Teaser**
- Social posts: "Something beautiful is coming to the UK 🌸" 
- Email blast to your database
- Instagram countdown sticker in stories

**Day 2 (Tuesday): The Problem**
- Video: "Why do beauty apps charge salons 35% commission? We think that's wrong."
- TikTok/Reel format, under 60 seconds
- End with "Tomorrow we show you the solution"

**Day 3 (Wednesday): THE LAUNCH**
- Full announcement video (2 minutes)
- Live demo of booking flow
- All social platforms simultaneously
- Press release to: Professional Beauty, Salon Magazine, Beauty Guild, NHBF
- Email: "We're live. Book your first treatment for FREE"

**Day 4 (Thursday): Social Proof**
- Salon owner testimonial videos (film 5 Founding Salons saying why they joined)
- "200 salons already on board" social proof counter

**Day 5 (Friday): Customer Incentive**
- "First 1,000 customers get £10 off their first booking"
- Referral code system activated: "Give £5, Get £5"

**Day 6 (Saturday): Influencer Surge** (see Section 2.2)

**Day 7 (Sunday): Recap & Results**
- "What a week" post with stats
- Number of bookings, salons joined, customers signed up

### 2.2 — Influencer Blitz (Launch Week)

**Budget: £2,000-5,000**

Target: 20-30 UK beauty micro-influencers (10K-50K followers)
Cost: £100-300 each for one TikTok/Reel

**The brief:**
1. Show them booking a real treatment through the app
2. Film the treatment itself (before/after)
3. Mention: "I found this on @onetouchbeauty — it's like Fresha but way cheaper for salons"
4. Include booking link in bio

**Why micro-influencers:**
- 10K-50K followers get 3-7% engagement (vs 1-2% for mega influencers)
- Beauty is the #1 category for influencer impact on purchase decisions
- £150 per creator × 30 creators = £4,500 for potentially 500K-1.5M impressions

**Where to find them:**
- Search TikTok: #UKbeauty #salonlife #beautytok #nailsofinstagram
- Use platforms: Collabstr, The Dots, or just DM directly
- Target creators who are themselves beauty professionals (double impact: they're both influencer AND potential salon sign-up)

### 2.3 — Launch PR

**Free PR targets:**
- Professional Beauty magazine (the industry bible)
- Salon Magazine
- The Beauty Guild
- NHBF newsletter
- Local press in your area (Rugby Advertiser, Coventry Telegraph, etc.)
- StartUp Donut, Startups.co.uk (startup press)

**Angle:** "UK entrepreneurs launch beauty marketplace that charges a third of Fresha's commission — giving power back to independent beauty professionals"

**Why journalists will care:**
- "David vs Goliath" — small UK startup vs £640M-valued Fresha
- Timely: UK beauty industry contributing £30.4B to GDP
- Human interest: local founders building a national platform
- Industry pain point: 75% of salons say commission costs are their #1 concern

## Phase 3: GROWTH ENGINE (Months 4-8)
### "Pour fuel on the fire"

### 3.1 — Paid Advertising

**Monthly budget: £3,000-5,000** (scaling as revenue grows)

**Channel 1: Google Ads (40% of budget)**
- Keywords: "book hair appointment near me", "beauty salon [city]", "nail appointment today", "hairdresser near me"
- UK beauty CPC: £0.80-£3.00
- Target: 10-15% booking conversion rate
- Expected CPA: £8-15 per new customer
- Focus on high-intent "near me" and "book" keywords

**Channel 2: Meta (Instagram + Facebook) Ads (35% of budget)**
- Ad types: Reels ads, Stories ads, Carousel (before/after transformations)
- Targeting: Women 18-45, interested in beauty/self-care, within target cities
- Retargeting: Website visitors who didn't book
- Expected CPA: £5-12 per new customer
- Creative: UGC-style content (filmed on phone, authentic, not polished)

**Channel 3: TikTok Ads (25% of budget)**
- Spark Ads (boost organic creator content — highest ROI on TikTok)
- In-Feed ads with booking CTA
- Target: 18-35, beauty interest, UK
- TikTok CPM for beauty: £10-20
- Creative: "I just found the cheapest way to book beauty treatments in the UK" hook

**Scaling rule:** For every £1 spent, we need to see £3+ in booking value within 30 days. If a channel drops below 3:1 ROAS, cut it. If it's above 5:1, double the spend.

### 3.2 — Video Marketing Campaigns

This is where we differentiate from every competitor. Fresha and Treatwell do ZERO creative video marketing. The beauty industry is THE most visual industry in the world. Use that.

**Campaign 1: "Transformation Tuesdays" (Ongoing weekly series)**
- Film real transformations at partner salons (before → after)
- 30-60 second TikTok/Reels
- End screen: "Book this look on One Touch Beauty"
- Partner salons get free content for their own channels too (they'll love this)
- Cost: £0 (film on phone, salons provide the transformation)

**Campaign 2: "The £50 Challenge" (Viral potential)**
- Give 5 people £50 each to book treatments through the app
- Film their entire experience (booking → salon → results)
- Edit into 3-minute YouTube videos + cut-down TikToks
- This is essentially a long-form ad disguised as entertainment
- Budget: £250 per episode + editing costs
- Target: 1 per month, 12 episodes over the year

**Campaign 3: "Salon Stories" (Documentary-style series)**
- 5-10 minute YouTube docs profiling real salon owners
- Their journey, their passion, why they went independent
- Emotional, authentic, shareable
- Positions One Touch Beauty as the platform that CARES about salons (vs Fresha/Treatwell taking their money)
- Film 1 per month
- Budget: £200-500 per episode

**Campaign 4: "Blind Beauty" (Viral stunt campaign)**
- Customers book treatments without seeing the salon — they only see ratings and reviews
- Film their reaction when they arrive + the result
- High entertainment value, highly shareable
- Budget: £500 per episode, 4 episodes

**Campaign 5: "Rate My Treatment" (User-generated content engine)**
- In-app prompt: After every booking, ask customers to film a 15-second review
- Incentivise with loyalty points
- Curate the best into social content
- This creates an endless content machine at zero cost

**Campaign 6: "Swap Shop" (Cross-promotion)**
- Two salon owners try each other's services
- Film the experience
- Both salons' audiences see it
- Cross-pollination of followers

### 3.3 — Referral Program

**Customer referral:**
- "Give £5, Get £5" — both referrer and referee get £5 credit
- Average referral programs see 2-3% of users refer someone
- At 10,000 users, that's 200-300 new customers per month at £5 each = £1,000-1,500/month cost
- Each referred customer is worth £50+ in first booking alone

**Salon referral:**
- "Invite a fellow salon owner, both get 3 months commission-free"
- Salon owners know other salon owners — this is the most powerful B2B growth channel
- Costs us deferred commission, not cash

### 3.4 — SEO & Content Marketing

**Blog strategy (2 posts per week):**
- "Best salons in [city]" — target every major UK city (SEO gold)
- "How much does [treatment] cost in 2026?" — captures high-intent searchers
- "[Treatment] before and after" — image-heavy, shareable
- "How to find a good [hairdresser/nail tech/etc.]" — educational
- These rank in Google and drive free organic traffic for years

**SEO targets:**
- "Book salon near me" — massive search volume
- "Beauty salon [every UK city]" — 100+ pages
- "Best hairdresser [city]" — local SEO domination
- Expected timeline: 3-6 months to start ranking, 6-12 months for significant traffic

### 3.5 — Partnership Marketing

**Partnership 1: Wedding venues & planners**
- Beauty is the #1 wedding spend category after the venue
- Partner with 50 wedding venues to recommend One Touch Beauty for bridal parties
- Commission-share model: venue gets £5 for every bridal booking

**Partnership 2: Hotels & Airbnbs**
- Guests always want beauty services
- QR code in hotel rooms linking to local salons on the platform
- "Book a treatment during your stay" cards

**Partnership 3: Gyms & fitness studios**
- Post-workout beauty treatments (nails, facials, massage)
- Cross-promote to gym members via reception desk flyers + digital screens

**Partnership 4: Beauty colleges & training academies**
- New graduates need clients — list their discounted services on the platform
- We get ultra-low-cost treatments that attract budget-conscious customers
- Graduates get their first clients and reviews

4. Local Facebook/Instagram ads: £500 for 2 weeks
5. Local PR: city newspaper, local radio
6. Partner with 2-3 local wedding venues/hotels
7. Total per city: ~£1,500-2,500 launch cost

### 4.2 — City Ambassador Program

**Hire 10 part-time City Ambassadors** across the UK:
- Pay: Commission-based (£10 per salon sign-up + £1 per booking from their city)
- Role: Sign up salons, attend networking events, manage local relationships
- Profile: Beauty professionals, marketing students, or just well-connected locals
- This is how Uber scaled — local market managers who own their city

### 4.3 — B2B Sales for Premium Subscriptions

Once we have 1,000+ salons, hire a part-time salesperson to upsell:
- Business Pro (£29/month): Advanced analytics, priority listing, marketing tools
- Premium (£49/month): All features + dedicated account manager + featured listing
- Enterprise (custom): Multi-location salons, white-label options

**Target: 25% conversion from free to paid = 500 paying salons**
- 300 × £29/month = £8,700/month = £104,400/year
- 200 × £49/month = £9,800/month = £117,600/year
- **Total subscription revenue: £222,000/year**

## Phase 5: RETENTION & MONETISATION
### "Keep them coming back"

### 5.1 — Customer Retention Tactics

**Automated email/SMS sequences:**
1. **Post-booking:** "How was your treatment? Leave a review for 50 loyalty points"
2. **7 days later:** "Ready for your next appointment? Here's 10% off"
3. **30 days (no rebook):** "We miss you! Here's a personalised recommendation"
4. **Birthday:** "Happy birthday! 🎂 Here's £10 credit on us"
5. **Seasonal:** "Summer's coming — trending treatments near you"

**Loyalty program:**
- 1 point per £1 spent
- 100 points = £5 off next booking
- Tier system: Bronze → Silver → Gold → Platinum
- Gold members get early access to new salons and exclusive offers

**Push notifications (tasteful, not spammy):**
- "Your salon just added a new service you'll love"
- "Last-minute deal: 30% off at [salon] today at 3pm"
- "You're 20 points away from your next reward!"

### 5.2 — Salon Retention

Salons leave platforms when they feel they're paying too much for too little.

**Our retention advantages:**
- Lower commission than every competitor
- Free marketing tools (campaign builder, social templates)
- Actual relationship (WhatsApp support, not a chatbot)
- Data they can't get elsewhere (client insights, retention rates)
- The more bookings they get, the harder it is to leave

**Monthly salon newsletter:**
- Industry insights, trend data
- Tips to grow their business
- Feature a "Salon of the Month"
- Makes them feel part of a community, not just a number

## The Unfair Advantages

Why One Touch Beauty wins where others haven't:

1. **Price:** 10% commission vs 20-35%. That's not a small difference — it's a business-changing one for a salon making £50K/year.

2. **UK-only focus:** Fresha is spread across 120 countries. We focus entirely on the UK market. That means faster support, better local knowledge, more relevant features.

3. **The untapped majority:** 55-75% of UK beauty businesses don't use ANY booking platform. They're on Instagram DMs and WhatsApp. We're not stealing from Fresha — we're converting the massive unserved market.

4. **Content-first:** Neither Fresha nor Treatwell produce any meaningful content. The beauty industry is the most visual industry in the world and our competitors are invisible on TikTok and Instagram. That's insane. We own this gap.

5. **Community, not corporation:** Fresha is a $640M VC-backed company. Treatwell is owned by a Japanese conglomerate. We're two UK founders who actually talk to our salon partners on WhatsApp. That matters in an industry built on personal relationships.

6. **Both sides of the market:** We have the website (for customers searching on Google), the app (for repeat bookings), AND the business tools (SaaS for salon management). Most competitors only have one or two of these.
